The market followed the cycles up to new highs last week. Both the NASDAQ and the S&P have broken out of ascending triangles to new highs, confirmed by a new high in the NYSE advance-decline line. The usual measuring formula is that price will run to a point equal to the height of the formation that has been broken out of, in this case 1000 points for the NASDAQ 100. This sets a target of 9000 (8000+1000). That is 12.5% over the last quote. This lofty target may not be reached but is does set up an excellent reward-risk ratio. The story is the same for the S&P.
